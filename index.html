INTANGIBLE CULTURAL

Intangible cultural heritage , promoted by UNESCO , focuses on the part of culture that is physically intangible (2001).

UNESCO defines "intangible cultural heritage" as practices, representations, expressions, knowledge, skills and tools, objects, artifacts, and resulting cultural spaces that communities, groups, people, and in some cases individuals recognize as part of their cultural heritage.

The Historic City of Ayutthaya, founded in 1350, was the second capital of the Siamese Kingdom. ASEAN CITY OF CULTURE

Yogyakarta was picked as a City of Culture because of its unique character. It is a perfect reflection of the ASEAN identity, of a plural and inclusive society. It's also a perfect base for visiting Indonesias most important archaeological sites, Borobudur and Prambanan.

Yogyakarta is regarded as an important centre for classical Javanese fine arts and culture such as ballet, batik textiles, drama, literature, music, poetry, silversmithing, visual arts, and wayang puppetry. Renowned as a centre of Indonesian education, Yogyakarta is home to a large student population and dozens of schools and universities, including Gadjah Mada University, the country's largest institute of higher education and one of its most prestigious.
